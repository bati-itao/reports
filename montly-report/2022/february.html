IT Accessibility Office Monthly Summary for February 2022

  • 2253 accessibility requests resolved YTD.
  • ITAO received 222 requests this month, an increase of 16% compared to February 2021, and an increase of 35% compared to January 2022.
  • Requests for ICT assessments reached 19% this month.
  • 28 cerfitications and 19 pending from the International Association of Accessibility Professionnals (IAAP).

Pionner in accessibility

  • Accessibility Corner launched in IITB News to increase viewership and awareness on accessibility.
  • ACP team engaged Vendor Management Office and Legal teams to reflect ACA regulations for COTS vendor communication.
  • ACP Accessibility coaching service is changing the coding culture within ESDC developers.

Achievements

  • ITAO is now offering 3 courses with the College@ESDC;
  • Staffing of seven new resources for Strategy and Audit team ongoing and estimated completion in February.
  • ACP Info Sessions to Product owners of COTS/FOSS attended by 250 staff.
  • ACP coaches presented at the February DeVCoP meeting to over 176 attendees to promote accessible coding.
  • GABI solution for Xerox printer has been implemented, new clients to be added.

Challenges

  • New floor plan lacks sufficient office space for ACE employees - ongoing discussions with CFOB to modify the floor plans.
  • Integrating desktop accessibility into the RMP.
  • Delays implementing AT software to employees with disabilities.
  • Bottleneck in packaging AT software because of the lack of capacity in the packaging team.
  • GABI solution for Xerox printer has been implemented, new clients to be added.
  • ACP overview presented to DG that highlighted the progress to date, issues, risks and next steps to seek funding for major multi-year project.
  • Revised ICT Accessibility strategy and alignment with ESDC Accessibility Office (EAO) strategy.
  • ACP submitted multi-year Pressure funds - awaiting decision to continue the project for next fiscal year.

Roadblocks

  • Funding for ACP project for next four years.
  • Engaging Application Development and the COTS owners for their commitments to work on achieving compliance due to conflicting priorities.
